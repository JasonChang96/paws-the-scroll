# Demo Plan

## Goal

Create a 60-second recorded hackathon demo that presents Paws The Scroll as a personal, funny, AI-powered behavioral activation companion.

The demo should be a direct screen demo with talking-head narration. Avoid b-roll. The centerpiece is the full-screen cat interruption.

## Core Message

Getting a cat drastically transformed my mental health because she interrupted me. Paws The Scroll recreates that interruption on the desktop: a needy cat appears when device paralysis takes over and turns being stuck into one tiny act of care.

## Audience

Hackathon judges.

## Tone

Hackathon dramatic, personal, and funny. The cat should be memorable and a little demanding without sounding shaming.

## Demo Arc

1. Personal hook: getting a cat transformed my mental health because she interrupted the spiral.
2. Problem: when stuck in paralysis, another dashboard or productivity reminder does not help.
3. Product moment: Paws The Scroll appears over the desktop and demands care.
4. AI moment: OpenAI generates the cat portrait, personality, dialogue, and tiny task using the user's goals, environment, mobility, and boundaries.
5. Payoff: completing the tiny task calms the cat, updates its needs, and starts a longer loop where time away helps the cat become more independent.
6. Closing line: Paws The Scroll turns paralysis into one tiny act of care.

## 60-Second Script

Getting a cat drastically transformed my mental health.

Not because she gave me productivity tips. Because she interrupted me.

When I am stuck in paralysis, I do not need another dashboard telling me I failed. I need something small, immediate, and hard to ignore.

So I built Paws The Scroll: a desktop cat that interrupts device paralysis.

When I have been actively using my computer too long, the cat appears over my screen and demands care.

And because caring for the cat maps to caring for myself, the task is tiny on purpose: stand up, drink water, clear one thing from the desk, take one breath, start one step.

The cat's personality, portrait, dialogue, and tasks are generated with OpenAI, based on my goals, environment, mobility, and boundaries. So it can be dramatic without being unsafe or shaming.

For example, instead of "be productive," it says: "Human. You have become furniture. Stand up for ten seconds so I know you are alive."

Then when I do it, the cat calms down, its needs change, and over time it becomes more independent when I spend less time stuck on my computer.

Paws The Scroll is a behavioral activation companion disguised as a needy desktop cat.

It turns paralysis into one tiny act of care.

## Recording Beats

1. Start on talking head: "Getting a cat drastically transformed my mental health..."
2. Switch to screen share while narration continues.
3. Show a normal stuck-at-computer setup: browser, notes, cluttered tabs, or an avoided task.
4. Trigger the demo interruption with `Cmd + Ctrl + Opt + P`.
5. Show the full-screen cat overlay above the desktop.
6. Show a funny generated cat line and a tiny task.
7. Mention OpenAI while showing the generated portrait and task.
8. Click `I did it`.
9. Show the cat reaction, needs update, or dashboard for only a few seconds.
10. End on the app or talking head with the punchline.

## Demo Build Priorities

1. Reliable full-screen overlay trigger.
2. Polished interruption state with a strong cat portrait, funny dialogue, one tiny task, and clear actions.
3. Visible OpenAI usage through generated cat portrait, personality, dialogue, or task.
4. Immediate completion payoff after clicking `I did it`.
5. Minimal dashboard proof if time allows: mood, needs, tiny actions completed, and time-away reward.

## Recommended Final Line

Paws The Scroll: the cat that bullies you into caring for yourself, gently.
